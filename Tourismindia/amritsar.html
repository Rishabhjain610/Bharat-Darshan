अमृतसर

The Golden City

Destinations

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple is the holiest shrine in Sikhism, revered for its spiritual significance and stunning architecture. Surrounded by the Amrit Sarovar (holy pool), the temple’s golden façade is a mesmerizing sight. It is open to people of all faiths, and visitors can also enjoy the free community meal (langar) served daily. The tranquility of the temple complex is awe-inspiring.

Jallianwala Bagh

Jallianwala Bagh is a public garden that serves as a poignant reminder of one of the darkest days in Indian history. In 1919, British troops opened fire on a peaceful gathering, killing hundreds. The park has a memorial that honors the lives lost and still bears bullet marks on its walls. A museum on-site offers insights into the event and the independence movement.

Wagah Border

The Wagah Border is the only road crossing between India and Pakistan. Every evening, the border hosts the Beating Retreat Ceremony, where soldiers from both nations lower their flags in a high-energy, coordinated parade. The patriotic atmosphere, with crowds cheering for their country, makes this event a must-watch.

Partition Museum

The Partition Museum in Amritsar is dedicated to the stories and memories of the 1947 Partition of India. It contains photographs, documents, oral histories, and personal belongings of refugees affected by the mass migration. The museum offers a moving narrative of the human cost of one of the largest mass migrations in history.

Durgiana Temple

The Durgiana Temple, also known as Lakshmi Narayan Temple, is a Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Durga. Architecturally similar to the Golden Temple, it is surrounded by a sacred water tank. The temple is a popular pilgrimage site for Hindus and is especially vibrant during festivals like Diwali and Navratri.

Akal Takht

The Akal Takht, meaning "Throne of the Timeless One," is one of the five sacred Takhts of Sikhism. Situated within the Golden Temple complex, it serves as the supreme seat of Sikh temporal authority. This Takht symbolizes justice and was established by Guru Hargobind Sahib, the sixth Sikh Guru.

Maharaja Ranjit Singh Museum

The Maharaja Ranjit Singh Museum is dedicated to the life and legacy of the first Maharaja of the Sikh Empire. The museum is housed in the beautiful Summer Palace of Maharaja Ranjit Singh and displays weapons, armor, paintings, and manuscripts. Visitors can also enjoy a sound and light show depicting the Maharaja’s heroic exploits.

Gobindgarh Fort

Gobindgarh Fort, once a stronghold of Maharaja Ranjit Singh, is now a lively cultural complex. The fort offers museums, 7D shows, live performances, and heritage walks, showcasing Punjab’s rich history. The fort's architecture and historical significance make it a popular destination for history enthusiasts.

Ram Tirath Ashram

Believed to be the birthplace of Lord Ram’s sons, Luv and Kush, this ancient site is mentioned in the Ramayana. The temple complex also has a tank where pilgrims take holy dips. The site is especially significant during the Ram Tirath Fair, where thousands gather to seek blessings.

Hall Bazaar

One of the busiest shopping streets in Amritsar, Hall Bazaar is known for its traditional handicrafts, jewelry, electronics, and garments. The market is famous for selling authentic Punjabi juttis (footwear) and phulkari-embroidered clothes. The arched entrance to the bazaar adds a touch of Mughal architecture to the vibrant scene.

Khalsa College

Founded in 1892, Khalsa College is not just an academic institution but also a symbol of the Sikh Renaissance. The stunning Indo-Saracenic architecture is a fusion of Mughal, Sikh, and British styles. The college is renowned for its contributions to education and Sikh culture, making it a place of pride for the region.

Tarn Taran Sahib

Located about 22 kilometers from Amritsar, Tarn Taran Sahib is a major Sikh pilgrimage site. It is known for its Gurudwara with the largest sarovar (holy tank) among all Sikh shrines. The serene atmosphere and spiritual significance of the place attract devotees and tourists alike.

Sadda Pind

Sadda Pind is a cultural village that offers visitors a chance to experience traditional Punjabi village life. From folk dances, pottery, and handicrafts to delicious Punjabi food, the village is a celebration of Punjab’s vibrant culture. It’s an ideal place for those looking to immerse themselves in rural traditions.

Gurudwara Baba Atal Rai

The Gurudwara is dedicated to Baba Atal Rai, the son of Guru Hargobind Sahib. Standing nine stories tall, it offers breathtaking views of the surrounding city. The structure is unique for its architectural beauty and is a place of peace for worshippers and visitors.
