जयपुर

The Pink City

Destinations

Amber fort

Amber Fort is a majestic fort located on a hilltop, known for its artistic style, blending both Hindu and Mughal elements. The fort's intricate mirror work and stunning courtyards make it a must-visit. Elephant rides up to the fort add a royal touch to the experience.

Hawa Mahal

Also known as the Palace of Winds, Hawa Mahal is a five-story building with 953 small windows, designed to allow royal ladies to observe street festivities while remaining hidden. The honeycomb-like structure is a perfect blend of Rajput and Mughal architecture.

City Palace

Located in the heart of Jaipur, City Palace is a complex of courtyards, gardens, and buildings. It served as the seat of the Maharaja of Jaipur and now houses a museum showcasing royal costumes, artifacts, and weapons.

Jal Mahal

Jal Mahal is a stunning palace situated in the middle of Man Sagar Lake. Its unique location gives the illusion of a floating palace. Visitors can enjoy the serene surroundings and panoramic views, especially during sunset.

Nahargarh Fort

Nahargarh Fort offers a breathtaking view of Jaipur city. Built as a retreat, this fort was part of the city's defense ring. It's a popular spot for sunset views and has a charming, scenic café inside the fort complex.

Jaigarh Fort

Jaigarh Fort, known for housing the world's largest cannon on wheels, Jaivana, is a robust military structure. The fort is connected to Amer Fort and offers panoramic views of the Aravalli hills and Jaipur city.

Albert Hall Museum

The oldest museum in Rajasthan, Albert Hall Museum displays a rich collection of art, including metal sculptures, carpets, paintings, and Egyptian mummies. Its Indo-Saracenic architecture adds to its allure.

Galtaji Temple

Also known as the Monkey Temple, Galtaji is an ancient Hindu pilgrimage site. The temple complex is known for its natural springs and sacred kunds (water tanks), where devotees come to bathe.

Birla Mandir

Birla Mandir is a beautiful white marble temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Goddess Lakshmi. The temple's intricate carvings, serene atmosphere, and scenic location make it a peaceful place to visit.

Govind Dev Ji Temple

Located within the City Palace complex, this temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna and holds great religious significance. The temple's idol is believed to have been brought from Vrindavan and is revered by thousands of devotees.

Sisodia Rani Garden

This beautifully landscaped garden was built by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II for his queen, Sisodia Rani. The garden is adorned with fountains, pavilions, and murals depicting scenes from the life of Lord Krishna.

Jantar Mantar

Jantar Mantar is an astronomical observatory built by Maharaja Jai Singh II. It houses the world's largest stone sundial and offers insights into ancient astronomy, with instruments to measure time, track celestial bodies, and predict eclipses.

Central Park

Central Park is the largest park in Jaipur, offering a relaxing environment with lush greenery, walking tracks, and a musical fountain. The park also houses the tallest Indian national flag, making it a popular spot for locals and tourists alike.

Panna Meena Ka Kund

This stepwell is a hidden gem in Jaipur, known for its unique architecture and symmetry. The stepwell was historically used to store water and is now a peaceful spot for photography and exploration.
