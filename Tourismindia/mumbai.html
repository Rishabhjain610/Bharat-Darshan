Destinations

Elephanta Caves

Elephanta Caves, located near Mumbai, are ancient rock-cut temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. These caves, known for their stunning sculptures, date back to the 5th to 8th centuries.

CSMT

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CSMT), formerly known as Victoria Terminus, is a historic railway station in Mumbai, India. Renowned for its Gothic Revival architecture, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a major railway hub in Mumbai.

Taj Mahal Palace

The Taj Mahal Palace Hotel in Mumbai is an iconic luxury hotel known for its grand architecture and rich history. Opened in 1903, it stands as a symbol of elegance and hospitality, offering stunning views of the Arabian Sea and Gateway of India.

Gateway of India

An iconic landmark built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary in 1911, it overlooks the Arabian Sea and is a popular starting point for exploring Mumbai.

Marine Drive

Known as the "Queen's Necklace" for its illuminated night view, Marine Drive is a scenic boulevard perfect for a leisurely walk along the Arabian Sea with stunning cityscape views.

Juhu Beach

A popular beach destination in Mumbai, Juhu Beach is known for its lively atmosphere, street food stalls, and picturesque sunset views over the Arabian Sea.

Vastu Sangrahalaya

This museum showcases a diverse collection of artifacts, including ancient sculptures, paintings, and historical relics, housed in a stunning Indo-Saracenic architectural building

Haji Ali Dargah

Situated on a small island off the coast of Worli, this mosque and tomb are renowned for their beautiful architecture and spiritual significance, accessible via a causeway during low tide.

Sanjay Gandhi National Park

A green oasis in the city, this park offers a variety of wildlife, including leopards and deer, and features the Kanheri Caves, a series of ancient Buddhist rock-cut structures.

Global Vipassana Pagoda

Located near Gorai, this massive golden pagoda is dedicated to promoting peace through meditation. The structure is awe-inspiring, and the surrounding tranquility makes it an ideal spot for those seeking spiritual respite from Mumbai’s chaos.

Siddhivinayak Temple

One of Mumbai’s most famous and revered temples, Siddhivinayak Temple is dedicated to Lord Ganesha. Located in Prabhadevi, it attracts millions of devotees, including Bollywood celebrities and politicians. Despite its popularity, many may not know about the rich history of the temple, which dates back to 1801.

Sewri Fort

Built in 1680 by the British, Sewri Fort offers stunning views of the Mumbai harbor. Though small and lesser-known, the fort becomes a popular spot during winter when thousands of flamingos flock to the nearby mudflats, making it a haven for birdwatchers.

Mumbai Film City

Mumbai Film City, located in Goregaon, is a sprawling complex that serves as the hub of India's entertainment industry, especially Bollywood. It offers visitors a glimpse into the world of movie-making, with guided tours that showcase live sets, sound stages, and filming locations for popular TV shows and films

Gilbert Hill

A geological marvel, Gilbert Hill is a 200-foot tall monolithic column of black basalt rock formed millions of years ago. Climb the steps to the top for a panoramic view of Andheri and surrounding suburbs, and marvel at this natural wonder in the midst of a city.
